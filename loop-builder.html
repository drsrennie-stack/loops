Loop Builder

Build a new loop from images or a PDF. It outputs a ready folder and an updated custom-loops.js you push to GitHub.

This tool runs entirely in your browser. Nothing is uploaded anywhere. When you are done it downloads a ZIP to your computer.

1Name your loop

The id becomes the image folder name. Avoid the names already used: intro, tissues, skin, bone, skull, appendicular, boneaxial2.

2Add your slides

Drop your slide images here, or click to choose.
Put them in order: question, answer, question, answer…

Each station is two images: the question slide (arrows, no answers), then its answer slide.

Drop a PDF here, or click to choose.
A PowerPoint or Keynote exported to PDF works.

After it loads, tag each page below as Q (question), A (answer), or Skip (title slides or blank animation frames). The tool pairs each Q with the next A.

3Review pairs & type the answer keys

Each row is one station. Type the answer (what the student should say). Both structures can go in one line, e.g. "Iliac Crest; Acetabulum".

4Add to your existing loops (optional)

If you have made loops before, drop your current custom-loops.js here so this new loop is added to them instead of replacing them. First time? Skip this.

Drop custom-loops.js here (optional), or click.

5Download your loop

To publish: unzip it, then in your loops repo put the images/<id> folder inside images/, and replace custom-loops.js with the new one. Commit and push in GitHub Desktop. Your new loop card appears automatically.
